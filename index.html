Borigaram Agriculture Technical College

Borigaram Agriculture Technical College is a private Agriculture Technical College owned by the Friends on the Path, under the trustee of the Jogye Order of Korean Buddhism.

Get Started!

A Look at How We Begin

Borigaram Agriculture Technical College was formally established in 2016 on NACTE REG/SAT/028 after the official ceremony done by the Most Venerable President Jaseung of the Jogye Orders of Korean Buddhism on 5th September, 2016. The College has been mandated to deliver a course in Horticulture and Agriculture Production disciplines leading to the National Technical Award (NTA L6) of a Diploma in Horticulture and Agriculture Production. Short courses to farmers and youth surrounding the College and beyond are provided depending on the need.

The mission of the College is to provide comprehensive gender sensitivity and quality agricultural education and training to a broad segment of the population through teaching, research and public services in the field of agriculture.

The broad objective of establishing Borigaram Agriculture Technical College is to create in Tanzania a place where knowledge and skills are transferred from one generation to the other, a place where relevant knowledge, skills and attitudes are acquired to promote rural development through agricultural growth.

The organization structure consists of the Advisory Board, which is responsible for strategic decision making and policy matters, while most of the routine matters and decisions are made at lower levels. The College functions are performed by the principal assisted by the head of departments. There are four departments namely Academic, Farm, Administration and Student’s welfare. From these emerge sections and units to handle the specific functions.

Accomodation:

The students are accommodated in the College hostels. There are two hostels with the capacity of 240 students, 120 for female students and the remaining 120 for male students. Each hostel has 15 rooms, accommodating 8 students per room. Each student is provided with mattress, sheets and a blanket.

Religious Life:

Tanzania is a secular state, therefore, students have complete freedom of religious worship to any religion; as long as it is intended to promote the wellbeing of the student’s life and encourage positive religious values.

Community

All matters concerning student’s administration, welfare and discipline are stipulated in the College rules and regulations. The department of student’s welfare headed by the Dean of students is responsible for the student’s administration, and welfare services.
